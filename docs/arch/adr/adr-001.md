# ADR-001: Post-Rework Settling Posture

## Status
accepted

## Context
The seven-epic skill-system rework (v3.12.0–v3.18.0, 2026-07) concluded with
a post-rework /intuition audit (report: bd-cnp). Four of its tensions share a
root: structures deliberately (re)built during the rework show high recent
change rates, and the obvious "fixes" would restructure them before any
post-rework change data exists.

- T1: skills/brainstorming braids design partnership with pipeline artifact
  production (41 commits/6mo — but every rework epic deliberately edited it).
- T2: skills/using-hyper bundles routing catalog, hard gates, and
  session-start duties (rebuilt as one unit in RW6a by design).
- T8: CLAUDE.md and README.md restate pipeline semantics and co-change with
  the hottest skills (they serve cold readers who lack the skills on disk).
- T12: 41:1 change-rate ratio between pipeline skills and practice skills in
  one flat namespace (Brand shearing layers).

All four rate signals are dominated by the rework itself; restructuring now
would be reacting to construction noise.

## Decision
We will keep all four structures as shipped and treat their tensions as
accepted, pending empirical post-rework change rates. Re-examination trigger:
the next /intuition audit run after meaningful non-rework history has
accumulated — if pipeline-vs-practice rates still exceed the 5:1 shearing
threshold, or brainstorming/using-hyper keep absorbing edits from unrelated
changes, the corresponding split (T1), unbundling (T2), doc thinning (T8), or
layer partition (T12) gets its own brainstorm.

## Consequences
- Avoids fixes-that-fail: no restructuring driven by churn the restructuring
  itself would have caused.
- The four tensions remain live and visible (this ADR is their record);
  future audits skip re-litigating them unless the trigger fires.
- Accepts continued whole-file re-publishing of stable content (using-hyper
  injection, practice skills re-versioned with pipeline bumps) in the interim.
